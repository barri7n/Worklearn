Wild elephant electrocuted in Sharper (Bangladesh)
Prothom Alo
October 2, 2016

A wild elephant was electrocuted at Pangarh village in Jhenaigati upazila
on Saturday night as it came in touch with a live electric wire set by
locals to prevent wild elephant herd from damaging their crops.

Locals set the electric wire around their croplands to prevent wild
elephants from destroying their crops.

A herd of elephants invaded the village around 9:30 pm, said upazila
nirbahi officer of Jhenaigati Mohammad Selim Reza quoting locals.

At one stage, one of the elephants came in touch with the live electric
wire and died on the spot, he said.
