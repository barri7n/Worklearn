Elephant electrocuted (India)
MSN News
December 22, 2011

Erode (TN), Dec 22 (PTI) A female elephant was electrocuted near a village in Sathyamangalam forests, the second such incident in over a week, officials said today.

The carcass of the 15-year-old pachyderm was found yesterday near a farmer''s land in Pudupeerkadavu village where banana crops were being cultivated.
The farmer had put up an electric wire-fence to scare away elephants, forest officials said, adding, the elephant got eletrocuted when it tried to raid the crops.
A veterinarian, who examined the carcass, stated that death was caused due to electrocution. The farmer has been arrested, officials said.
On December 14, carcass of a 40-year-old female elephant was found in Chinnathalatti in Kadambur forest range. Its death was also due to electrocution and three persons, including a farmer, were arrested, officials said.
