Elephant electrocuted in Jharkhand (East Singhbhum District, India)
India.com
August 14, 2017

The elephant was electrocuted in Dhalbhumgarh in East Singhbhum district
when its trunk touched a 11,000 volt wire, according to police.

The elephant was in pain for more than 45 minutes before dying.

In Jharkhand, more than 25 elephants have died due to electrocution in the
past few years.

In another incident, a herd of elephants damaged over six houses in Malhaan
Panchayat in Latehar district.

More than 700 people have been killed by elephants in Jharkhand after the
creation of the state in 2000.
