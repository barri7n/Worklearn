Elephant electrocuted in Jamshedpur (India)
Business Standard
July 4, 2015

Jamshedpur: A full grown wild elephant was electrocuted in Mirzadih, around
25 kms from Jamshedpur under Patamda police station of East Singhbhum
district, forest department official said.
35-year-old male elephant came in contact with a live electric wire in the
wee hours on Saturday, which was hanging loose after a storm accompanied by
rain lashed the area on Friday, Forester Mangal Kashyap said.
Forest officials have arrived at the spot and process was on to bury the
pachyderm following a post-mortem, he said.
